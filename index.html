Adding Custom Songs

  1. Locate Your Mod Files: Navigate to the mod you are porting (e.g., "Have My Heart (Charted)").
  2. Copy Audio Files: Take the instrumental (Inst) and vocal (Voices) tracks from the mod. If the mod doesn't use vocal tracks, just copy the instrumental.
  3. Replace Existing Tracks: Go to assets/songs/bopeebo (or the folder of the song you are replacing). Replace the existing audio files with your custom tracks.

Adding Custom Charts

  1. Locate the Chart Files: Find the chart you want to port in the mod.
  2. Open the Mod: Open the mod you are porting.
  3. Access the Chart Editor: Load the song you are porting. Press 7 on your keyboard to open the chart editor.
  4. Change the Song Name: Navigate to the "Song" tab. Change the song name from a song from "Tutorial - Week 7" to your custom song. Click "Save".
  5. Select Difficulty: Use charts from "Easy," "Normal," and "Hard." (Custom difficulties are in development.) Save the charts in the assets/data folder.
  6. Playtest in Sourceless Engine: Open Sourceless Engine. Playtest your chart to ensure everything works correctly.

FAQ

What is FNF Sourceless Engine?
Sourceless Engine is a Friday Night Funkin' (FNF) Engine developed without using the source code.
Why did you make it without source?
The main reason for creating Sourceless Engine without source code is because I prefer not to use VS Code, which is typically required for projects involving direct source code modification on Windows.
Can I use this in my own mod?
Yes, you are allowed to use Sourceless Engine in your own mods as long as you credit the creator.
What mods are you working on?
Currently, I am using Sourceless Engine for developing W-Sides Remixes and Charizard's Wrath mods.
